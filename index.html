PulseForge is an iOS workout tracking application developed and operated by TrueNorthTactics ("we", "us", or "our"). This Privacy & Security Policy explains how PulseForge handles information when you use the app.

We have designed PulseForge from the ground up with a privacy-first architecture. The simplest summary: your health and workout data belongs to you, lives on your devices, and is never monetised.

HealthKit Data â€” Special Protections

Apple requires us to be explicit: PulseForge will never use HealthKit data for advertising, never sell it to data brokers, and will never share it with any third party without your explicit, informed consent.

All HealthKit reads and writes happen locally on your iPhone or Apple Watch. When HealthKit sync is enabled, PulseForge reads data from Apple's Health app to enrich your workout metrics (heart rate zones, intensity score, Progress Pulse). It also writes completed workouts back to Health so your activity rings and health records remain accurate.

HealthKit data is processed entirely on-device. It is never uploaded to PulseForge servers, never included in analytics payloads, and never shared with third-party SDKs.

You can revoke HealthKit permissions at any time in iOS Settings â†’ Privacy & Security â†’ Health â†’ PulseForge. Revoking access disables the relevant metric features but does not affect your workout history stored in PulseForge's own database.

09

Security Measures

We take security seriously. PulseForge relies exclusively on Apple platform security:

Encryption at rest: All on-device data is encrypted by iOS when your device is locked. iCloud data uses Apple's end-to-end encryption for private containers.

No custom backend: Because there is no PulseForge server storing your health data, there is no PulseForge server to breach.

App Transport Security: All network communication (e.g., subscription validation) uses HTTPS with ATS enforced.
